<p><p>Foam insulation is a type of insulation material made from polymer foams, such as polyurethane, polystyrene, or polyisocyanurate. It is widely used in residential, commercial, and industrial applications to improve energy efficiency by reducing heat transfer. Foam insulation is available in various forms, including spray foam, rigid foam boards, and foam-in-place, offering versatility and high thermal resistance. Its lightweight, durable, and moisture-resistant properties make it a preferred choice for insulation in walls, roofs, and foundations.</p><p>The Foam Insulation Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.9% during the forecast period. The construction industry's expansion, particularly in emerging economies, is also fueling market growth. Additionally, advancements in foam insulation technologies, such as eco-friendly and low-GWP (Global Warming Potential) materials, are gaining traction.</p><p>Key trends in the market include the adoption of spray foam insulation due to its superior sealing properties and the growing preference for green building certifications like LEED. However, challenges such as fluctuating raw material prices and environmental concerns related to certain foam types may impact the market. Overall, the foam insulation market is poised for steady growth, supported by innovation and increasing focus on energy conservation.</p></p>

Polystyrene foam, available as expanded (EPS) or extruded (XPS), provides excellent moisture resistance and is commonly used in construction.

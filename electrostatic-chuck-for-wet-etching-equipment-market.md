<p><p>Electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are critical components in wet etching equipment, enhancing the process of holding wafers securely during etching. The Coulomb type utilizes electrostatic forces generated by charged plates to adhere the wafer, providing strong hold and minimal damage. In contrast, the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type employs a novel principle based on induced polarization, which allows for a more uniform and adjustable grip. Both types cater to varying needs in semiconductor fabrication, impacting efficiency and yield in the manufacturing process.</p></p>

<p><p>The photovoltaic polysilicon market is segmented into four types based on the physical form of the polysilicon produced. Granular polysilicon consists of small, round particles, facilitating better flow and handling during manufacturing. Blocky polysilicon features larger, cube-like structures, providing high purity and consistent material properties. Flaky polysilicon has thin, flat shapes that enhance surface area, beneficial for certain applications. Powdered polysilicon, a fine particulate form, allows for easy integration into diverse manufacturing processes, enhancing versatility in solar cell production.</p></p>

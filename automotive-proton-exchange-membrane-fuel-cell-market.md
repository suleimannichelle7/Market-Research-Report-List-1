<p><p>The Automotive Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell (PEMFC) market encompasses three key types of hydrogen storage: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen involves storing hydrogen at high pressures in tanks, making it widely used in vehicles. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen is stored at very low temperatures, optimizing density, but requiring complex handling. Hydrides utilize metal-based compounds to absorb and release hydrogen, offering safer storage but typically with lower energy density. Each type has distinct advantages and applications in fuel cell vehicles.</p></p>

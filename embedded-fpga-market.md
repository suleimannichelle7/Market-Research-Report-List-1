<p><p>The Embedded FPGA market comprises various types, including EEPROM, Antifuse, SRAM, Flash, and Others. EEPROMs provide reprogrammability and retain data without power, making them suitable for applications needing frequent updates. Antifuse technology offers high security and is ideal for applications requiring a one-time configuration. SRAM FPGAs provide high speed and flexibility but are volatile. Flash-based FPGAs combine non-volatile memory with programmable capabilities, allowing for multiple configurations. Other types include specialized variations to meet specific industry needs.</p></p>

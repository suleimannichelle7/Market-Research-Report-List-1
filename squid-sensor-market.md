<p><p>The SQUID sensor market is divided into two main types: low-temperature SQUIDs and high-temperature SQUIDs. Low-temperature SQUIDs operate effectively at cryogenic temperatures, typically near absolute zero, making them ideal for precision measurements in scientific and medical applications. In contrast, high-temperature SQUIDs function at relatively warmer temperatures, allowing for broader applications in industrial settings, such as power grid monitoring and magnetic field imaging. Both types leverage superconductivity for high sensitivity and accurate magnetic field detection.</p></p>

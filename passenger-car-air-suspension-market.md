<p><p>The Passenger Car Air Suspension market includes two main types: Electronically Controlled Air Suspension (ECAS) and Non-Electronically Controlled Air Suspension. ECAS uses sensors and electronic systems to automatically adjust ride height and stiffness, offering enhanced comfort, stability, and adaptability to road conditions. Non-Electronically Controlled systems rely on manual or mechanical adjustments, providing basic air suspension benefits like improved ride quality but without automated features. ECAS is more advanced and costly, while non-ECAS is simpler and more affordable. Both cater to varying consumer needs for comfort and performance.</p></p>

<p><p>The microbial biosurfactants market is primarily categorized into three types: rhamnolipids, sophorolipids, and others. Rhamnolipids are glycolipid surfactants produced by certain bacteria, known for their effectiveness in solubilizing hydrophobic compounds. Sophorolipids, derived from yeast, are valued for their biodegradability and environmental safety. The "others" segment includes various biosurfactants like lipopeptides and mannosylerythritol lipids, which find applications in agriculture, food, and pharmaceuticals, driven by their eco-friendly properties and multifunctional uses.</p></p>

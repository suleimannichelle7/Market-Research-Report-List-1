<p><p>Antifibrinolytic drugs are used to prevent excessive bleeding by inhibiting the fibrinolysis process. Key types include Aminocaproic Acid and Tranexamic Acid, available in both oral and injectable forms, which stabilize clot formation. Aprotinin and Trasylol (another name for Aprotinin) reduce blood loss during surgery. Cyklokapron is specifically used for bleeding in surgical procedures, while Lysteda is an oral option aimed at treating heavy menstrual bleeding. Fibrinogen and Riastap play roles in promoting clotting by providing necessary proteins for fibrin formation.</p></p>

<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are distinguished into multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs) and single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs), both holding significant potential for electricity generation applications. MWCNTs consist of several concentric tubes, offering enhanced electrical conductivity and structural strength, making them suitable for various electronic devices. Conversely, SWCNTs, featuring a single tubular layer, demonstrate exceptional electrical properties and high current-carrying capacity, ideal for nanoscale applications. Both types are explored for use in energy storage systems, solar cells, and advanced electrical materials.</p></p>
